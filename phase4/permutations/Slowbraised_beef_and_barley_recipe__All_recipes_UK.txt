========
1. Put the beef in a bowl with the garlic , bay leaves , juniper berries and thyme.
2. Pour over the wine , then cover and leave to marinate in the fridge for 8 hours or overnight.
3. The next day , preheat the oven to 160 C ( 325F , gas mark 3 ).
4. Put the button onions in a bowl and pour over enough boiling water to cover.
5. Leave for 2 minutes , then drain.
6. When cool enough to handle , peel off the skins.
7. Set the onions aside.
8. Remove the beef from the marinade and pat dry on kitchen paper.
9. Heat the oil in a large flameproof casserole over a moderately high heat.
10. Add the beef and brown on all sides.
11. Do this in batches , if necessary , so the pan is not overcrowded.
12. Remove the beef from the casserole and set aside on a plate.
13. Add the onions to the casserole and cook gently for 3-4 minutes or until lightly coloured all over.
14. Add the barley and cook for 1 minute , stirring , then return the beef and any beefy juices to the casserole.
15. Pour in the stock and bring to a simmer.
16. Strain the marinade into the casserole , and add the bay leaves and sprig of thyme.
17. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
18. Cover with a tight-fitting lid , transfer to the oven and braise for 45 minutes.
19. Add the carrots , celery and swede , and stir to mix.
20. Cover again and braise for a further 1-1¼ hours or until the beef , barley and vegetables are tender.
21. Remove the bay leaves and thyme stalk before serving.
