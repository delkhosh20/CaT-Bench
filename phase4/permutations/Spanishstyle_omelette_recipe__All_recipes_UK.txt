1. Bring a large pan of lightly salted water to the boil.
2. Add the potatoes and peas and return to the boil.
3. Reduce the heat and simmer for 3 minutes or until the potatoes are just beginning to soften.
4. Drain and set aside.
5. Heat 2 tbsp of the oil in a 25cm frying pan with a flameproof handle.
6. When the oil is hot , reduce the heat to medium , add the onion and cook for 2 minutes.
7. Add the garlic and continue cooking for a further 2-3 minutes until the onion is soft , but not brown.
8. Add the potatoes and peas to the pan and continue frying , stirring , for 5 minutes or until the potatoes are tender.
9. Remove the pan from the heat.
10. In a large bowl , lightly beat the eggs with the parsley and a little black pepper , then stir in the ham , the potato , onion and pea mixture and the tomatoes.
11. Place the frying pan back over a medium heat , add the remaining oil and swirl it around.
12. When the oil is hot , pour in the egg mixture , spreading it out evenly.
13. Cook the omelette for 5-7 minutes on a low heat , shaking the pan frequently , until the base is set.
14. Use a spatula to loosen and lift the edges of the omelette , allowing the uncooked egg mixture to run underneath.
15. Meanwhile preheat the grill to medium.
16. Place the pan under the grill and continue cooking the omelette for 5 minutes until it is golden brown and set.
17. Pierce the top with a knife to make sure it is cooked all the way through.
18. Slide the omelette out of the pan onto a board and leave to cool for 2-3 minutes.
19. Serve cut into wedges.
